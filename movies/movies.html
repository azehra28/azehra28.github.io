Yeh Jawaani Hai Deewani (YJHD)

YJHD hits my sweet spot: friendships that feel real, travel that opens you up and exposes you the reality of the world, and the tug of war between ambition and love. In my opinion, it’s cozy, colorful, and somehow always energizing on a rewatch (I have seen it 10 times at this point).

Why I Love It

The film balances wanderlust and home so well. Bunny’s drive to see the world mirrors my own pull toward new challenges, while Naina’s grounded warmth reminds me why roots matter, even more relatable living in a foreign country. The music and friendships feel like real life. They are messy, funny, yet loyal no matter how long it has been.

Favorite Moments

  • Manali trek: This trek to the mountains made people go from strangers to friends. The banter, mishaps, bonding truly brought everyone close together and let them make memories for a lifetime.
  • Ghagra & Badtameez Dil:This song was full of pure joy, color, and choreography.
  • New Year’s confession: This moment highlighted quiet honesty after all the noise.Bunny returned to Naina to confess his feelings and live happily ever after together.
